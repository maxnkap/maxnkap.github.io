 Washington University in St. Louis
Senior Design Project

Our design problem was to create a solar radiation tracking device. The primary goal of this device was to keep a solar panel perpendicular to the solar radiation with 95% efficiency throughout the day. We were able to build a sun tracking solar panel, untethered from an electrical grid, that charged the battery faster than it was run down.

Initial concept drawing of a counterweighted panel.

First Solidworks CAD to flush out the concepts and ensure reasonable sizes. Click here for a 3D view of this CAD. With appreciation to Max Kapczynski for his CAD work.

From here, we were ready to start building the control code. Fortunately we only needed a very simple loop (where we compared two photoresistors and rotated towards the one reporting a brighter light), and a friendly Arduino board.

We wrote some simple code and discovered we needed to redesign a few things, in order to get the solar panel to work consistently. We also had to reconsider our motor choices. We had anticipated a higher voltage driving the motors we had originally chosen, but had to get a higher gear ratio motor to be able to rotate the panel. Ultimately we were able to get the motors consistently driving the panel (a short video) and we had a final hardware design. (3d view of this CAD)

From here it was just a problem of cleaning up the wiring, and some final testing to make sure everything was still working, before tweaking the code for better performance. Once we had a smoothly working mechanical system (another short video), we were able to test the thresholds on the photoresistors and we were ready to decorate!
